Review is the bottleneck.

What hone is

It reads your code, not just your diff.

hone stages each series at its exact base-commit in a real kernel worktree and runs an agentic review that walks the call graph and reads whole functions — surfacing concrete, code-level concerns. It won't catch everything today, but it follows a versioned, revisable methodology and gets sharper over time.

Architecture

A pipeline that fits your workflow.

Benefit 01 — new developer workflow

Feedback before you hit send.

Upload a series — or let hone pull yours straight from lore — and get a full, code-aware review in minutes, not days. Triage the concerns, iterate, send a cleaner v2. It won't catch everything — but every contributor gets a fast, consistent first pass, and it improves with every revision.

The first-patch problem

The bar to a first kernel contribution is high, and early mistakes play out in public — on the lists, under your company's domain, permanently archived. hone is the private dry-run: catch the obvious problems before a developer's first patch ever reaches the mailing list.

Benefit 02 — scale

Throughput is just node count.

A hone-node claims a task, stages its own kernel worktree, reviews, and submits — then claims the next. Each is token-budgeted, self-healing, and reports its health. Add nodes and review capacity scales horizontally. One node or fifty, same pipeline.

Benefit 04 — revise the checks

Your methodology, versioned.

The review methodology is a living document — principles, stages, and a set of bug-class checks you can add, tune, or retire. Every version is tracked, coverage is measured, and hone learns from real maintainer comments to propose new checks. Tighten review the same way you tighten code.
